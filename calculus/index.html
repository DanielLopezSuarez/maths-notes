    Calculus Formulas

    Key formulas for differentiation and integration.

    1. Definition of Derivative

    The fundamental limit definition of a derivative.

    \[ f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \]

    2. Power Rule

    The basic rule for differentiating powers of \(x\).

    \[ \frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = n \cdot x^{n-1} \]

    3. Product Rule

    Used when differentiating two functions multiplied together.

    \[ \frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x) \]

    4. Quotient Rule

    Used when differentiating one function divided by another.

    \[ \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right] = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \]

    5. Chain Rule

    Used for differentiating composite functions.

    \[ \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x) \]

    6. Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

    Relates differentiation and integration.

    \[ \int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a) \]

    7. Integration by Parts

    The product rule in reverse for integration.

    \[ \int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du \]

    8. L'HÃ´pital's Rule

    Used to evaluate limits that result in indeterminate forms like \(0/0\).

    \[ \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} \]

    9. Maclaurin Series for \(e^x\)

    Expansion of the exponential function.

    \[ e^x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!} = 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^3}{3!} + \dots \]

    10. Mean Value Theorem

    States there is a tangent parallel to the secant line.

    \[ f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \]
